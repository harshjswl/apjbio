A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, born on October 15, 1931, in Tamil Nadu, was an acclaimed Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India (2002-2007). Known as the "Missile Man of India," he was pivotal in developing India's missile and nuclear programs, including the Pokhran-II tests in 1998. As President, he was beloved for his humility and focus on education, earning the title "People's President." After his presidency, Kalam continued to inspire youth until his passing on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong, leaving behind a legacy of innovation and patriotism.

Biographies

  • Born: October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India
  • Education: Studied aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology
  • Key Roles: Worked at DRDO and ISRO; developed India's missile programs
  • Nuclear Tests: Key figure in the Pokhran-II tests (1998)
  • President: Served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007
  • Nickname: "Missile Man of India"
  • Awards: Bharat Ratna (1997), Padma Bhushan (1981), Padma Vibhushan (1990)
  • Death: Passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong
